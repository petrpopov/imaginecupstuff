From what I found until now there seems to be one thread for each dll loaded so if you are still doing computations when it's your turn to move then your turn will be skipped (my pathfinder currently take the 1500 turns for the first move and even in this time it doesn't find a path to its goal, lol).
I don't really know how much time we have to do our computations btw.

So you are not limited in time consumption for your algorithm but you better not take too much time ;)

Just for info, all the experiments I have done with abstraction (taking nxn blocks and pathfinding on them) give slight improvements to overall time to find paths.

However, mine 'only' takes 53 turns to analyse the map and all the routes I want at startup without any abstraction.  I am working to improve this, obviously!

At the moment my pathfinder is close to optimal in most cases.  Unfortunately it is somewhat less than optimal for my explorers, who like to do a bit of freelance exploring far from the madding crowd.  They still find the enemy in plenty of time, however, but are definately not doing what I meant them to be doing!!
