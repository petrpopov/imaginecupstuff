Here is what i think after having take a look to the engine of the project:

When a nanobot arrives on a cell, a counter, NbrTourInCell is initialized to the speed in the Cell (getSpeedFromAreaType). While the value of the counter is more than 0, the nanobot stays in the cell. By example in low density regions, the nanobot stays one turn before its next move.

The blood stream is here to adjust the time nanobots stay in cells. By example if a bot tries to go downstream(or upstream), the threshold becomes 2(or -2) instead of 0, so the nanobot can move again when the counter is below 2 (or -2)!

In the case where the speed of the region is less or equals 2 (like in low density one), the bot doesn't wait between moves if it goes downstream.

Perpendicular streams are not taken into account, so i will buy new eyes and launch another simulation too see it practically.

Hope this will help you!

Guillaume.
