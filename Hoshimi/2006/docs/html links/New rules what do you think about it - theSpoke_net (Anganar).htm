This year, for those how already dowloaded the SDK, there is new rules, new challenges. The main change is the apparition of mission.

On a map (tissue), you have a mission to accomplish. a mission is composed of differents objectives. Objectives can be:

  • NavigationObjective: you must go to different point on the map, with a kind (or any kind) of nanobots at a specific round with (or not with) enough amount of AZN. For example:

Go to point (40, 30) between turn 50 and 60 with 30 AZN in stock and to point (60,50) between turn 70 and 100 with 40 AZN in stock with a NanoExplorer.

Or more easier:

Go to point (40, 30) between turn 50 and turn 1400 with any kind of nanobot.

  • NeuroControlerObjective: enemy team must have x nanoneurocontrolers alive at turn y.
  • ScoreObjective: you must have a score of x at turn y.
  • AIAliveObjective: your NanoAI must be alive at turn x.

In comparaison to last year, you can consider that last year first mission was composed of two missions:

  • AIAlive: your nanoAI must be alive at turn 1499,
  • ScoreObjective: you must have 300 points at turn 1499.

As you can see, missions can be more complicated, more sophisticated. So your AI must be more accurate and can be specific for a kind of mission.

That's why I think, last year competitors don't have really a big advantage (but it is only my opinion).

As last year, SDK is not sealed for next rounds, if you think more differents kinds of objectives can be implemented, tell me. If you find a bug or ideas for new specifications, tell me. I'll enjoy improving the SDK.

Please, tell me what you think about this.
